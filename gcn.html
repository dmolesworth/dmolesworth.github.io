About

Growing a cycling community

Global Cycling Network (GCN) is the world’s largest online cycling channel. The GCN app is a hub for cycling enthusiasts, offering news, videos, live racing, feature films, and a community for cyclists.

Problem space

The GCN app’s social experience lacks product-market fit. Users who come to watch races don’t stay, with week-one retention averaging just 16%.

New users currently have no control over the content they see on day one, making engagement difficult. To improve retention, we need to refine the user experience and surface more relevant content from the start.

How might we make our app experience more relevant & engaging for new users?

Measures of success

Users gain a clearer/quicker understanding of the product's value proposition. Users are retained more easily meaning a larger user base to convert to paying subscribers

  • Increase week 1 retention
  • Increase MAU

Existing new user journey

The existing new user experience was suboptimal, overwhelming users with cycling content that may not have been relevant to them, ultimately giving them little reason to return;

  1. Downloads app
  2. Lands on Home page
  3. Watches live/on-demand content or completes other job
  4. Exits app

It became clear that improving the first-time user experience was crucial to maximizing retention.

Team ideation

I ran sessions with the team and stakeholders to explore ways to improve the new user experience from day one, with the goal of increasing retention.

Following the ideation sessions, I developed a user testing plan to validate our initial prototypes. This involved defining key research objectives, selecting test participants, and designing scenarios to assess how new users interacted with the proposed improvements.

The goal was to gather actionable insights on whether these changes made the app experience more engaging and relevant from day one.

Hypotheses to validate

  1. Giving users the opportunity to tell us what they’re interested in will result in a more engaging experience because the content they see will be more relevant to them from day one
  2. Allowing users to follow brands & High Quality Contributors when they first visit the app will result in more relevant engaging content from day one
  3. By changing the home page experience to display relevant, user-selected tags, we aim to make the content in the home feed feel more personalized and engaging to each user.

Learnings: Making selections

  • Users felt like they would be “trapped” in their initial selections without any way to undo.
    So we removed 'What brings you to GCN' screen and added copy to interests selection to reassure that interests could be edited

  • Users were hesitant when they reached the interests screen, unsure of what to do.
    So we added icons to the interests to make the selections more intuitive

Learnings: Following brands

  • We saw a lot of confusion and some negative sentiment on the “People” screen. Users didn’t know what following would do and suspected marketing tactics from brands.
    We decided to introduce this content later in the journey. By embedding brand following into the home page feed, users could better understand the type of content available to them.

Learnings: Managing interests

    So we explored and tested a few different ideas around helping users manage their interests, eventually landing on managing and exploring interests as part of the search feature

Beta users

As well as moderated user testing, we posted an unmoderated test link to our beta users to get additional insight and feedback on our prototype.

We first rolled out the feature to our engaged beta users, who provided positive feedback along with areas for improvement. One of the themes of feedback was how users managed their interests. One user suggested, 'I think I’d manage these interests in my profile, so I could add and remove things in a central place

Impact

We saw a 92% increase in week-one retention, rising from 16% to 31%. This improvement was accompanied by a correlated increase in monthly active users (exact figure TBC). However, despite these gains, both metrics remain below industry standards for week-one retention, highlighting the need for further iteration and optimization.

For future iterations, we enhanced the social feed by incorporating diverse content types. This allows users to discover relevant content more easily, explore their interests, and connect with others.

We made these changes to better target and engage existing users, ensuring they can easily discover relevant content and deepen their connection with the app.
